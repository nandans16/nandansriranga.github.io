About

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering at Syracuse University, advised by Prof. Pramod K. Varshney, with expected completion in October 2026. My research focuses on statistical signal processing, sequential inference, distributed detection, adaptive sensing, sensor fusion, passive radar, anomaly detection, and machine-learning-based analysis of sensor data.

I am especially interested in research engineering and applied scientist roles where mathematical modeling, computational experimentation, first-principles debugging, and reliable evaluation matter. I have also used Mathpix during my PhD workflow to convert equations into LaTeX, so I have first-hand appreciation for mathematical OCR and document-understanding problems.

Selected Projects

Sequential Universal Two-Sample Testing

Developed adaptive and active-sensing tests for comparing two unknown data-generating distributions without assuming parametric likelihood models. This work connects to online model evaluation, distribution-shift detection, and adaptive data collection.

Universal testing Adaptive sensing Model evaluation
Online Sensor Collaboration for Moving Target Detection

Developed adaptive collaboration algorithms for distributed moving-target detection. Built MATLAB simulation pipelines for Monte Carlo experiments, detection probability, false-alarm analysis, and topology tradeoffs.

MATLAB parfor GLRT
Quickest Detection of Link Outages

Developed low-delay statistical detectors for identifying abrupt link failures and sparse heterogeneous anomalies in wireless sensor networks. Relevant to time-series anomaly detection and sensor-network monitoring.

Change detection Anomaly detection Network monitoring
Passive Radar Target Detection

Developed distributed passive radar detection methods using whitening, subspace transformations, cross-correlation, and receiver collaboration under communication constraints.

Passive radar Subspace methods Sensor fusion
